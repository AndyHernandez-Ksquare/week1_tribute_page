5 things you did know about Nikola Tesla

  • Tesla was a Serbian-American inventor, electrical engineer, mechanical engineer, and futurist. He was born on July 10, 1856, in what is now modern-day Croatia, and he passed away on January 7, 1943, in New York City.
  • Tesla is most famous for his work with alternating current (AC) electrical systems. He invented the Tesla coil, which is still used in radio technology today.
  • In 1887, Tesla developed an induction motor that ran on alternating current. This motor is widely considered to be one of his greatest achievements.
  • Tesla was known for his eccentric behavior, including his aversion to germs and his habit of working from 3 a.m. until 11 p.m.
  • Tesla was a polyglot and spoke eight languages fluently, including Serbo-Croatian, English, Czech, French, German, Hungarian, Italian, and Latin.
